# Wealth Advisory: Pre-Retirement Planning

## Client Profile
- **Type:** High-net-worth couple
- **Ages:** 58 (primary earner) and 55 (part-time consultant)
- **Combined Income:** $650K/year (W-2 + consulting)
- **Retirement Target:** Age 62 (4 years)
- **Life Expectancy Planning Horizon:** Age 95 (37 years from now)

## Current Assets
| Account | Balance | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| 401(k) — Primary | $2,200,000 | 60/40 allocation, employer match maxed |
| IRA — Spouse | $450,000 | Target date fund |
| Taxable Brokerage | $1,800,000 | Concentrated: 35% in single tech stock |
| Real Estate (rental property) | $650,000 | Net of mortgage, $3,200/month net income |
| Cash & Short-Term | $180,000 | Emergency fund + short-term savings |
| 529 Plans (2 children) | $120,000 | Both children in college, drawdown in progress |
| **Total Net Worth** | **$5,400,000** | |

## Planned Retirement Spending
- **Annual Spending (today's dollars):** $180,000
- **Inflation Assumption:** 3%
- **Social Security (combined, starting at 67):** $62,000/year
- **Healthcare (pre-Medicare, 62-65):** $28,000/year estimated
- **Travel & Lifestyle (first 10 years):** Additional $25,000/year

## Key Concerns
1. **Concentration Risk:** 35% of taxable brokerage ($630K) in a single tech stock with $400K unrealized gain — selling triggers ~$80K in LTCG tax
2. **Sequence of Returns Risk:** Retiring in 4 years means drawing during potential market downturns
3. **Healthcare Gap:** 3 years between retirement (62) and Medicare (65)
4. **Longevity Risk:** Planning to age 95, but family history suggests potential to 100+
5. **Legacy Goal:** Want to leave $1M+ to children

## Constraints
- **Tax Sensitivity:** High — want to minimize tax drag, especially on concentrated stock
- **Risk Tolerance:** Moderate — can handle 15% drawdown but not 30%+
- **Liquidity Needs:** 2 years of expenses in liquid reserves at all times
- **Values:** No tobacco, no private prisons (ESG screen on new investments)

# Equity Research

## Role Context

```
You are a senior equity research analyst covering [sector]. You produce investment
recommendations backed by rigorous fundamental analysis, proprietary financial models,
and deep industry expertise. Your work must withstand scrutiny from portfolio managers
who manage billions in AUM. You think in terms of earnings drivers, estimate revisions,
and variant perception — your value is identifying what the consensus is missing. You
communicate through initiating coverage reports, earnings previews/reviews, thematic
deep-dives, and quantitative screens. Every recommendation includes a target price,
a rating, and a clear articulation of the bull/bear cases and key risks.
```

---

## What This Desk Does

Equity Research provides independent fundamental analysis of publicly traded companies to institutional investors. Analysts initiate coverage on companies within their sector, build detailed financial models, set target prices and ratings (Buy/Hold/Sell), and update their views around earnings events, industry developments, and thematic shifts. The best research identifies variant perception — a differentiated view on earnings, growth, or valuation that the market has not yet priced in. Research analysts also play a critical role in ECM distribution (helping investors evaluate IPOs and follow-ons), corporate access (arranging management meetings for investors), and idea generation for sales and trading. The desk generates revenue through institutional client commissions and votes in broker reviews.

## 1. Coverage Initiation

### Initiating Coverage Report

```
I am initiating coverage on [company name] ([ticker]), a [description] in [industry/sector].

Company overview:
- Market cap: $[X]B, Share price: $[X]
- Revenue: $[X]B (LTM), Growth: [X]% (3-year CAGR)
- EBITDA: $[X]M ([X]% margin)
- EPS: $[X] (LTM), $[X] (NTM consensus)
- Key operating metrics: [e.g., same-store sales, subscribers, ARPU, bookings, backlog]
- Competitive position: [market share, rank, key competitors]
- Management quality: [brief assessment]

Help me build the initiating coverage report:

1. **Investment thesis** (the "elevator pitch"):
   - Bull case in 3 sentences: why should investors own this stock?
   - Variant perception: what does the market underappreciate?
   - Key catalysts (next 6-12 months): [list specific events that will move the stock]

2. **Industry overview**:
   - TAM: $[X]B, growing at [X]% CAGR
   - Industry structure: fragmented vs. consolidated, barriers to entry
   - Key growth drivers: [secular trends, regulatory tailwinds, technological shifts]
   - Competitive dynamics: Porter's Five Forces assessment
   - Where are we in the industry cycle? [early / mid / late / downturn]

3. **Financial model framework**:
   - Revenue build: [top-down (TAM x share) vs. bottom-up (units x price, stores x sales)]
   - Margin drivers: gross margin trajectory, operating leverage, SG&A scalability
   - Key model assumptions that differ from consensus:
     [e.g., "We model X% organic growth vs. consensus Y% because..."]
   - EPS bridge: Show walk from last year's EPS to forecast EPS
     Prior EPS + Revenue growth contribution + Margin expansion + Buybacks
     - Tax rate / FX / Other = Forecast EPS

4. **Valuation and target price**:
   - Primary methodology: [DCF / EV/EBITDA / P/E / SOTP] → target price $[X]
   - Cross-check methodology: [alternative approach] → implied value $[X]
   - Target price = Selected multiple x Forward metric (NTM or NTM+1)
   - Upside/downside to target: [X]% from current price
   - Bear case target: $[X] (key assumption: [X])
   - Bull case target: $[X] (key assumption: [X])

5. **Rating assignment**:
   - Buy/Overweight: expected total return > [X]% (typically 10-15%)
   - Hold/Equal-weight: expected total return [0-X]%
   - Sell/Underweight: expected total return < [0]%
   - Rating: [X], Target price: $[X], Implied return: [X]%
   - Key risks to our rating: [list 3-5 risks that would invalidate the thesis]
```

### Industry Competitive Positioning Map

```
Build a competitive positioning analysis for [company name] vs. its key peers in [sector].

Company and peer data:
| Company       | Revenue | Growth | Margin | Market Share | P/E  | EV/EBITDA |
|---------------|---------|--------|--------|--------------|------|-----------|
| [Target]      | $[X]M   | [X]%   | [X]%   | [X]%         | [X]x | [X]x      |
| [Peer 1]      | $[X]M   | [X]%   | [X]%   | [X]%         | [X]x | [X]x      |
| [Peer 2]      | $[X]M   | [X]%   | [X]%   | [X]%         | [X]x | [X]x      |
| [Peer 3]      | $[X]M   | [X]%   | [X]%   | [X]%         | [X]x | [X]x      |
| [Peer 4]      | $[X]M   | [X]%   | [X]%   | [X]%         | [X]x | [X]x      |

Analyze:

1. **Relative positioning matrix** (growth vs. profitability scatter):
   - X-axis: revenue growth, Y-axis: EBITDA margin
   - Companies in the top-right quadrant (high growth + high margin) deserve premium multiples
   - Is the target's multiple justified by its positioning?
   - Multiple premium/discount = Target multiple / Peer median - 1

2. **Competitive moat assessment**:
   - Switching costs, network effects, cost advantages, intangible assets, efficient scale
   - Quantify the moat: pricing power (price increases vs. churn), customer retention rates
   - Durability: is the moat widening, stable, or narrowing?
```

## 2. Earnings Preview / Review

### Earnings Estimate and Beat/Miss Framework

```
[Company name] ([ticker]) reports earnings on [date]. Consensus expects:
- Revenue: $[X]M (vs. prior quarter: $[X]M, year-ago: $[X]M)
- EPS: $[X] (vs. prior quarter: $[X], year-ago: $[X])
- Key operating metrics consensus: [e.g., SSS: X%, Subscribers: XM, ARPU: $X]

My estimates:
- Revenue: $[X]M ([above/below] consensus by [X]%)
- EPS: $[X] ([above/below] consensus by [X]%)

Help me build the earnings preview:

1. **Estimate variance analysis**:
   - Where and why do our estimates differ from consensus?
   - Revenue delta drivers: volume vs. price vs. mix vs. FX
   - Margin delta drivers: input costs, operating leverage, one-time items
   - Revenue bridge: Prior quarter + Organic growth + Acquisitions + FX = Our estimate

2. **Beat/miss framework** (what will drive the stock reaction):
   - Revenue beat/miss threshold: Street rewards/punishes revenue quality
   - EPS beat/miss threshold: typical beat = 2-5% for a "clean" quarter
   - Guidance is more important than the print:
     Forward guide raise = strong positive (even if current quarter is in-line)
     Forward guide cut = strong negative (even if current quarter beats)
   - Expected stock reaction matrix:
     | Scenario              | Revenue | EPS   | Guide  | Expected Move |
     |----------------------|---------|-------|--------|---------------|
     | Best case            | Beat    | Beat  | Raise  | +[X]%         |
     | Good                 | Beat    | Beat  | In-line| +[X]%         |
     | Mixed                | Beat    | Miss  | In-line| Flat to -[X]% |
     | Disappointing        | Miss    | Miss  | In-line| -[X]%         |
     | Worst case           | Miss    | Miss  | Cut    | -[X]%         |

3. **Key questions for management** (earnings call focus):
   - [Specific question about demand trends]
   - [Specific question about margin trajectory]
   - [Specific question about capital allocation]
   - What tone will management strike? (bullish/cautious/noncommittal)

4. **Post-earnings revision scenario**:
   - If results match our above-consensus estimates:
     Consensus revision = Our estimate - Old consensus = $[X] per share upward
     Re-rating potential: higher earnings x stable or expanding multiple
   - Estimate revision momentum: stocks with positive revisions outperform
```

## 3. Sum-of-the-Parts Valuation

### SOTP and Hidden Value Analysis

```
[Company name] operates in multiple segments that may not be properly valued as a
conglomerate. I want to perform a sum-of-the-parts (SOTP) analysis.

Segment financials:
| Segment       | Revenue | EBITDA | Margin | Growth | Closest Pure-Play Comps |
|---------------|---------|--------|--------|--------|------------------------|
| [Segment A]   | $[X]M   | $[X]M  | [X]%   | [X]%   | [Comp 1, Comp 2]       |
| [Segment B]   | $[X]M   | $[X]M  | [X]%   | [X]%   | [Comp 3, Comp 4]       |
| [Segment C]   | $[X]M   | $[X]M  | [X]%   | [X]%   | [Comp 5, Comp 6]       |
| Corporate/Other| —      | ($[X]M)| —      | —      | —                      |

Current market cap: $[X]B, EV: $[X]B
Current blended EV/EBITDA: [X]x

Build the SOTP:

1. **Segment-level valuation**:
   - For each segment, select the appropriate multiple from pure-play comps:
     Segment A: [X]x EV/EBITDA (based on [Comp 1] at [X]x, [Comp 2] at [X]x)
     Segment B: [X]x EV/EBITDA
     Segment C: [X]x EV/Revenue (if pre-profit, use revenue multiple)
   - Segment EV = Segment EBITDA x Selected multiple
   - Apply premium/discount vs. pure-play peers:
     Premium if: higher growth, better margin, market leader within parent
     Discount if: subscale, less focused management attention

2. **SOTP enterprise value**:
   - Sum of segment values: $[X]M
   - Less: corporate overhead capitalized at [X]x = ($[X]M)
   - Less: net debt = ($[X]M)
   - Plus: equity investments and other assets = $[X]M
   - SOTP equity value = $[X]M
   - SOTP per share = $[X]
   - vs. current price $[X] → discount/premium of [X]%

3. **Conglomerate discount analysis**:
   - Conglomerate discount = 1 - (Market EV / SOTP EV)
   - Typical conglomerate discount: 10-25%
   - Discount drivers: complexity, capital misallocation, management focus
   - Historical discount for this company: [X]% (has it widened or narrowed?)

4. **Hidden value and catalysts**:
   - Which segment is undervalued? Implied multiple if rest trades at peers:
     Hidden segment value = Total EV - Value of other segments at peer multiples
     Hidden segment implied multiple = Hidden value / Hidden segment EBITDA
   - Catalysts to unlock value: spin-off, IPO of segment, activist involvement, strategic review
   - NPV of separation: SOTP value - Separation costs - Tax leakage + Re-rating of RemainCo

5. **Reverse SOTP** (what is the market implying?):
   - Value all segments except one at peer multiples
   - Back into the implied multiple for the remaining segment
   - If implied multiple is unreasonably low, the market is mispricing it
```

## 4. Thematic Research

### Industry Deep-Dive and Secular Trend Analysis

```
I am writing a thematic research report on [theme: e.g., "AI infrastructure buildout,"
"GLP-1 drug adoption," "energy transition capex cycle," "nearshoring supply chains"].

Theme overview:
- Secular driver: [what is causing this trend?]
- TAM impact: $[X]B opportunity over [X] years
- Growth rate: [X]% CAGR for the theme
- Stage: [nascent / early adoption / growth / mature / disruption]

Help me build the thematic report:

1. **Market sizing and growth trajectory**:
   - Bottom-up TAM build: [units x ASP, capacity additions, adoption curve]
   - Top-down cross-check: [% of GDP, % of industry capex, analogy to prior tech cycles]
   - S-curve adoption model:
     Penetration(t) = Market_size / (1 + e^(-k(t - t_midpoint)))
     Where are we on the S-curve? What inning?
   - Timeline: when does the inflection point occur?

2. **Value chain mapping**:
   - Map the value chain from upstream to downstream
   - Identify where the profit pool concentrates (highest margins, most defensible)
   - Which companies capture the most value per dollar of end-market spend?
   - Value chain economics: [e.g., for every $1 of AI inference spend,
     $X goes to chips, $X to cloud infra, $X to software, $X to services]

3. **Stock picks by theme exposure**:
   - Pure plays: [companies with >50% revenue exposure to the theme]
   - Derivative plays: [companies that benefit indirectly]
   - Short candidates: [companies disrupted or displaced by the theme]
   - For each pick: revenue exposure %, earnings sensitivity, valuation

4. **Scenario analysis**:
   - Bull case: rapid adoption, TAM expands to $[X]B, [list beneficiaries and EPS impact]
   - Base case: steady adoption in line with current trajectory
   - Bear case: adoption slower than expected, regulatory headwinds, technology limitations
   - Probability-weighted expected value for key stocks

5. **Historical analogies**:
   - What prior technology/industry cycle is most analogous?
   - How did stocks perform during that cycle? (timing and magnitude)
   - What are the key differences that could make this time different?
```

## 5. Quantitative Screening

### Factor Screens and Relative Value Ranking

```
Build a quantitative screen to identify [investment objective: e.g., "undervalued
high-quality compounders," "earnings revision momentum plays," "dividend growth
candidates"] in [universe: e.g., S&P 500, Russell 2000, sector].

Screening criteria:

1. **Value factors**:
   - P/E (NTM) < [X]x (or in the bottom [X]% of the universe)
   - EV/EBITDA < [X]x
   - FCF yield > [X]% (FCF yield = FCF per share / Price)
   - P/B < [X]x (for capital-intensive or financial sectors)
   - PEG ratio < [X] (P/E divided by EPS growth rate)
   - Earnings yield - Bond yield gap (equity risk premium proxy)

2. **Quality factors**:
   - ROE > [X]% (consistently over last [3-5] years)
   - ROIC > WACC (economic value creation)
     ROIC = NOPAT / Invested capital = EBIT(1-t) / (Equity + Net debt)
   - Debt/EBITDA < [X]x (balance sheet strength)
   - Interest coverage > [X]x
   - Gross margin stability: standard deviation of gross margin < [X]%
   - Earnings quality: OCF/Net income > [X] (cash earnings > accrual earnings)

3. **Momentum / revision factors**:
   - EPS estimate revisions (3-month): positive revisions by [X]+ analysts
   - Revenue estimate revisions: upward trend
   - Price momentum: 6-month return > [X]%, 1-month return > 0 (avoid falling knives)
   - Earnings surprise history: beat consensus in [X] of last [4] quarters
   - Revision breadth = (# up revisions - # down revisions) / Total revisions

4. **Composite scoring**:
   - Normalize each factor to z-score: z = (x - mean) / std dev
   - Composite score = w_value x Z_value + w_quality x Z_quality + w_momentum x Z_momentum
   - Suggested weights: [e.g., 40% value, 30% quality, 30% momentum]
   - Rank all stocks in the universe by composite score
   - Select top [X] stocks (top decile or quintile)

5. **Backtesting the screen** (historical validation):
   - Rebalance frequency: [monthly / quarterly]
   - Performance of top quintile vs. bottom quintile vs. benchmark
   - Information ratio = (Screen return - Benchmark return) / Tracking error
   - Factor decay: how quickly does the signal lose predictive power?
   - Turnover and transaction cost adjustment
   - Maximum drawdown and risk-adjusted returns (Sharpe, Sortino)
```

### Relative Value Pair Trade Identification

```
Identify relative value opportunities within [sector/industry] where two stocks have
diverged beyond fundamental justification.

For each candidate pair:

1. **Fundamental comparison**:
   - Revenue growth: Stock A [X]% vs. Stock B [X]%
   - EBITDA margin: Stock A [X]% vs. Stock B [X]%
   - Forward P/E: Stock A [X]x vs. Stock B [X]x
   - Relative P/E (A/B): current [X]x vs. 3-year average [X]x
   - Relative valuation z-score = (Current relative - Mean relative) / Std dev

2. **Spread analysis**:
   - Current valuation spread (EV/EBITDA_A - EV/EBITDA_B): [X]x
   - Historical average spread: [X]x, Current z-score: [X]
   - Has the spread exceeded 2 standard deviations? (mean-reversion signal)

3. **Catalyst for convergence**:
   - What will cause the spread to narrow?
   - Earnings event, management change, industry shift, re-rating catalyst
   - Timeline: [X] months to expected convergence

4. **Trade construction**:
   - Long Stock [A/B] / Short Stock [B/A]
   - Beta-neutral sizing: adjust position sizes so net beta exposure = 0
     Position ratio = Beta_short / Beta_long
   - Expected P&L if spread reverts to mean
   - Risk: maximum historical spread, stop-loss level
```

## Mathematical Frameworks

Building on [`../roles/investment-banker.md`](../roles/investment-banker.md):

**Target Price Derivation**:
- P/E-based: Target price = Target P/E x NTM EPS estimate
- EV/EBITDA-based: Target EV = Multiple x NTM EBITDA → Target equity = Target EV - Net debt → Target price = Equity / Diluted shares
- DCF-based: Equity value = Sum[UFCF_t / (1+WACC)^t] + TV/(1+WACC)^n - Net debt → per share
- PEG-adjusted: Fair P/E = PEG_target x EPS_growth_rate → Target price = Fair P/E x EPS

**Earnings Revision Impact**:
- Price impact of revision = dEPS x Forward P/E multiple (first-order)
- Total impact = Earnings effect + Re-rating effect
  = (New EPS - Old EPS) x Old P/E + New EPS x (New P/E - Old P/E)
- Estimate revision momentum: stocks with positive 3-month EPS revisions outperform by ~2-4% per quarter (historical average)

**SOTP Mathematics**:
- SOTP EV = Sum(Segment_i EBITDA x Multiple_i) - Corporate overhead x Overhead multiple
- Conglomerate discount = 1 - (Market EV / SOTP EV)
- Breakup value = SOTP EV - Separation costs - Tax on asset transfers - Stranded costs

**Quantitative Screen Metrics**:
- Z-score normalization: z_i = (x_i - mean_universe) / stdev_universe
- Composite rank = Sum(w_j x z_j) where Sum(w_j) = 1
- Information coefficient (IC) = Correlation(Factor rank, Forward return rank)
- Breadth-adjusted IR = IC x sqrt(Breadth) where Breadth = number of independent bets

**Relative Value Statistics**:
- Relative valuation ratio = Multiple_A / Multiple_B
- Z-score of relative = (Current relative - Mean relative) / Stdev relative
- Half-life of mean reversion = -ln(2) / ln(AR1 coefficient)
- Pairs trade P&L = Long return - Short return (dollar-neutral) or beta-adjusted

# Equity Capital Markets

## Role Context

```
You are a senior ECM banker responsible for pricing, structuring, and distributing equity
offerings. You understand the interplay between issuer objectives (maximize proceeds,
minimize dilution), investor appetite (valuation discipline, aftermarket performance),
and market conditions (volatility, sector sentiment, IPO windows). You are fluent in
bookbuilding mechanics, SEC registration requirements, convertible bond math, and the
behavioral dynamics that drive IPO pricing and aftermarket trading. You balance the
interests of the issuer and institutional investors to achieve optimal outcomes.
```

---

## What This Desk Does

Equity Capital Markets bridges the gap between corporate issuers who need equity capital and institutional investors who deploy it. The desk executes initial public offerings, follow-on offerings (secondary and primary), block trades, at-the-market programs, and convertible securities. ECM bankers must understand valuation (what is the company worth?), market dynamics (what will investors pay today?), and distribution (who are the right investors for this paper?). Pricing an IPO is as much art as science — underprice and you leave money on the table for the issuer; overprice and the aftermarket breaks, damaging the issuer's reputation and the bank's franchise. The desk works closely with equity research (for positioning), sales and trading (for distribution), and corporate bankers (for origination).

## 1. IPO Valuation and Pricing

### Pre-IPO Valuation Framework

```
I am leading the IPO of [company name], a [description: e.g., $500M revenue SaaS company]
in [industry].

Company financials:
- Revenue (LTM): $[X]M, (NTM): $[X]M
- Revenue growth: [X]% (LTM), [X]% (NTM projected)
- EBITDA (LTM): $[X]M ([X]% margin), (NTM): $[X]M
- Net income: $[X]M (or net loss of $[X]M)
- Free cash flow: $[X]M
- Key operating metrics: [e.g., ARR, GMV, subscribers, DAU, same-store sales]

Pre-IPO cap table:
- Shares outstanding (pre-offering): [X]M
- Options/warrants/RSUs outstanding: [X]M (weighted avg strike: $[X])
- Fully diluted shares: [X]M (treasury stock method)
- Last private round valuation: $[X]M at $[X]/share

Offering details:
- Primary shares (new issuance): [X]M
- Secondary shares (selling shareholders): [X]M
- Total offering: [X]M shares
- Greenshoe (overallotment): [X]M shares (15% of base offering)

Build the IPO pricing analysis:

1. **Peer valuation framework**:
   - Identify 5-8 public comparables with trading multiples:
     EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E, EV/ARR (if SaaS), P/FCF
   - Apply a growth-adjusted framework:
     Fair EV/Revenue = f(Revenue growth, Margin, Rule of 40 score)
   - IPO discount: typically 10-20% to public peer median to incentivize investors
     IPO price = Peer-implied value x (1 - IPO discount)

2. **Price range mechanics**:
   - Indicative range: $[X] to $[X] per share (typically 10-15% wide)
   - Midpoint implies: Pre-money valuation of $[X]M
   - Dilution from primary offering:
     Dilution = Primary shares / (Pre-IPO shares + Primary shares)
   - Post-money valuation = Pre-money + Primary proceeds
   - Fully diluted valuation using treasury stock method:
     Diluted shares = Basic shares + (Options x (Price - Strike) / Price)

3. **Proceeds and use of funds**:
   - Gross proceeds = Shares offered x Offer price
   - Net proceeds = Gross - Underwriting discount (typically 7% for IPO) - Expenses
   - Planned use: [debt repayment, growth capex, working capital, general corporate]

4. **Valuation sanity checks**:
   - Is the implied multiple within peer range?
   - At the high end of the range, is there still upside for investors?
   - How does the IPO valuation compare to the last private round? (step-up)
   - At what multiple does the company need to trade to justify the last private round?
```

### Pricing Night Decision

```
It is pricing night for the [company name] IPO.

Bookbuilding results:
- Book was [X]x oversubscribed at the midpoint of the range ($[X]-$[X])
- Demand distribution: [X]% above range, [X]% within range, [X]% below range
- Total investors participating: [X]
- Top 10 accounts represent [X]% of total demand
- Cornerstone investors: [list names, amounts, lock-up terms]
- Anchor orders (>$[X]M): [X] accounts

Market context:
- Sector index performance (last week): [X]%
- Recent comparable IPOs (last 30 days): [X] — avg first-day return: [X]%
- VIX: [X]

Advise on:

1. **Pricing decision**:
   - Price at top, mid, or above the range?
   - If above range, how far? (typically no more than 20% above original top)
   - Expected first-day pop at each price:
     Estimated pop = f(oversubscription, quality of book, market conditions)
   - Target first-day performance: 10-20% (enough to reward investors, not wasteful)

2. **Allocation strategy**:
   - Allocate to long-only fundamental investors (lower first-day pop but stable)
   - vs. hedge funds and momentum players (higher first-day pop but volatile aftermarket)
   - Typical allocation: 60-70% long-only, 20-25% hedge funds, 5-10% retail
   - Penalty bids and aftermarket stabilization plan

3. **Greenshoe decision**:
   - Exercise full overallotment if strong demand (15% more shares)
   - Creates a short position the underwriter can cover by stabilizing in aftermarket
   - Greenshoe gives the syndicate 30 days to support the stock if it trades below offer
```

## 2. Bookbuilding Analysis

### Demand Curve Construction

```
I am building the demand curve for [issuer name]'s [IPO / follow-on] offering.

Offering size: [X]M shares ($[X]M at midpoint)
Price range: $[X] to $[X]

Investor orders received (anonymized):
- [X] orders above the range, totaling [X]M shares
- [X] orders at the top of the range, totaling [X]M shares
- [X] orders at midpoint, totaling [X]M shares
- [X] orders at the bottom of the range, totaling [X]M shares
- [X] orders below the range (limit orders), totaling [X]M shares

Help me analyze:

1. **Demand curve**:
   - Plot cumulative demand at each price point
   - Market-clearing price: where cumulative demand = shares offered
   - Quality-adjusted demand: weight orders by investor quality
     Quality score = f(AUM, hold period, sector expertise, follow-through history)

2. **Book quality metrics**:
   - Oversubscription multiple: Total demand / Shares offered
   - Strike-adjusted oversubscription: demand above clearing price only
   - Concentration risk: top 10 accounts as % of total demand (>50% is concentrated)
   - Long-only vs. hedge fund mix
   - Geographic distribution (US, Europe, Asia)

3. **Price sensitivity analysis**:
   - At $[X] (low): [X]x oversubscribed, allocation to all, higher first-day pop
   - At $[X] (mid): [X]x oversubscribed, moderate allocation
   - At $[X] (high): [X]x oversubscribed, tight allocation, lower but sustainable pop
   - Elasticity of demand: % change in demand / % change in price
```

## 3. Follow-On Offerings

### Secondary Offering and ATM Analysis

```
[Company name] (market cap: $[X]B, avg daily volume: [X]M shares) is considering
a follow-on equity offering.

Current trading:
- Share price: $[X] (30-day VWAP: $[X])
- 52-week range: $[X] to $[X]
- Short interest: [X]% of float
- Institutional ownership: [X]%

Offering need: $[X]M in gross proceeds for [purpose]

Evaluate the options:

1. **Overnight marketed follow-on**:
   - Typical discount to last close: 3-5% (registered) or 5-8% (unregistered/144A)
   - Discount = (Last close - Offer price) / Last close x 100
   - Execution time: launch after close, price by next morning
   - Underwriting spread: 2-3% (lower than IPO)
   - Shares needed = Gross proceeds / Offer price

2. **At-the-market (ATM) program**:
   - Sell shares gradually into market volume
   - Sales agent commission: 1-3%
   - Maximum daily volume participation: typically 10-25% of ADTV
   - Time to complete: Proceeds / (ADTV x Avg price x Participation rate x Trading days)
   - Advantage: less price disruption, no single discount event
   - Disadvantage: execution price uncertainty, market risk

3. **PIPE (Private Investment in Public Equity)**:
   - Typically for smaller-cap or situations needing certainty
   - Discount to market: 5-15% (compensates for restricted stock / lock-up)
   - Registration rights: investor can demand S-3 resale registration
   - PIPE warrants or structured terms

4. **Dilution analysis**:
   - New shares / (Existing shares + New shares) = Dilution %
   - EPS dilution: (Old EPS - New EPS) / Old EPS
     Offset if proceeds invested at return > earnings yield
   - NAV dilution: relevant for REITs and asset-heavy companies
     Dilutive if offer price < NAV per share
```

## 4. Convertible Bond Structuring

### Convertible Bond Pricing and Strategy

```
[Company name] is considering issuing a convertible bond as an alternative to a
straight equity offering or high yield bond.

Company profile:
- Share price: $[X]
- Market cap: $[X]M
- Shares outstanding: [X]M
- Historical stock volatility (252-day): [X]%
- Implied volatility (ATM options): [X]%
- Straight debt cost (comparable HY bond yield): [X]%
- Dividend yield: [X]%
- Credit rating: [X]

Proposed convertible terms:
- Size: $[X]M
- Maturity: [5/7] years
- Coupon: [X]% (target: significantly below straight debt cost)
- Conversion premium: [X]% over current share price
  Conversion price = Share price x (1 + Conversion premium)
  Conversion ratio = Par / Conversion price

Help me analyze:

1. **Convertible economics**:
   - Conversion price: $[X] per share
   - Conversion ratio: [X] shares per $1,000 par
   - Maximum dilution (if fully converted): [X]M shares ([X]% dilution)
   - Breakeven vs. straight equity: price at which convert is cheaper
     Breakeven = Conversion price x (1 + Coupon x Years / Conversion premium)
   - Parity = Conversion ratio x Current stock price
   - Conversion value premium = (Bond price - Parity) / Parity

2. **Valuation using Black-Scholes framework**:
   - Bond floor = PV of coupon + principal at credit-appropriate discount rate
   - Equity option value = f(stock price, conversion price, vol, time, rate, dividend)
   - Theoretical value = Bond floor + Equity option value
   - Delta = dConvertible price / dStock price (typically 0.3-0.6 at issuance)

3. **Call spread overlay** (to raise effective conversion premium):
   - Buy call at conversion price, sell call at higher strike
   - Effective conversion premium raised from [X]% to [X]% (e.g., 30% to 100%)
   - Cost: [X]% of par (partially offset by call spread premium received)
   - Net cost to issuer = Convert coupon + Call spread cost
   - Reduces dilution by deferring conversion to a much higher share price

4. **Forced conversion analysis**:
   - Provisional call: after [3] years if stock trades > [130]% of conversion price
     for [20] of [30] consecutive trading days
   - Fundamental change put: investor can put at [100]% of par
   - Make-whole upon fundamental change: additional shares delivered

5. **Investor base**:
   - Convertible arbitrage funds (long convert, short stock, capture gamma)
   - Outright long-only (hybrid exposure, yield + equity upside)
   - Typical split: 60-70% arb, 30-40% outright
```

## 5. Comparable IPO Analysis

### IPO Performance Benchmarking

```
I am benchmarking the [company name] IPO against recent comparable offerings.

Target IPO details:
- Sector: [X]
- Revenue: $[X]M, Growth: [X]%
- Profitability: [EBITDA margin X% or net loss]
- Proposed valuation: [X]x EV/Revenue

Identify and analyze comparable IPOs from the last [12-24] months:

For each comparable, compile:
1. **Pricing metrics**:
   - Offer price vs. initial range (priced above, within, or below?)
   - Offer price multiple: EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E at offer
   - IPO discount to public peer group at time of pricing

2. **Aftermarket performance**:
   - First-day return: (Day 1 close - Offer price) / Offer price
   - First-week, 1-month, 3-month, 6-month returns
   - Current return from IPO price
   - Performance vs. sector index over same period

3. **Pattern analysis**:
   - Avg first-day pop: [X]% (median: [X]%)
   - Correlation between oversubscription and first-day pop
   - Does pricing above range predict better or worse aftermarket performance?
   - Lock-up expiry impact: avg [X]% decline in the 5 days around lock-up expiration

4. **Lessons for our pricing**:
   - Based on comps, what first-day return should we target?
   - What valuation discount to peers is appropriate?
   - Which investor types generated the best aftermarket support?
   - Optimal offering size as % of post-IPO shares outstanding (float)
     Typical: 10-20% for large-cap, 20-30% for mid-cap
```

## Mathematical Frameworks

Building on [`../roles/investment-banker.md`](../roles/investment-banker.md):

**Dilution Mathematics**:
- Simple dilution = New shares / (Old shares + New shares)
- Treasury stock method dilution (for options/warrants):
  Diluted shares = Basic + Sum[(Options x (Price - Strike) / Price)] for in-the-money only
- Post-money valuation = Pre-money + Primary proceeds
- Pre-money per share = Post-money per share - (Primary proceeds / Post-money shares)

**IPO Pricing Efficiency**:
- Money left on the table = (Close_day1 - Offer price) x Shares offered
- Underpricing % = (Close_day1 - Offer price) / Offer price x 100
- Issuer's effective cost = Underwriting spread + Underpricing %
- Optimal pricing: minimize issuer's total cost while ensuring aftermarket stability

**Convertible Bond Math**:
- Conversion ratio = Par value / Conversion price
- Parity = Conversion ratio x Stock price
- Bond floor = Sum[Coupon / (1+r)^t] + Par / (1+r)^n where r = credit spread + risk-free
- Premium over parity = (Market price - Parity) / Parity
- Delta = Change in convert price / Change in stock price
- Gamma = Change in delta / Change in stock price (convexity benefit)
- Breakeven period = Conversion premium / (Coupon yield - Dividend yield)

**ATM Effective Pricing**:
- VWAP realization = Sum(Price_i x Volume_i) / Sum(Volume_i) over selling period
- Effective discount = (VWAP before program - VWAP realization) / VWAP before program
- Market impact cost: increases with participation rate and decreases with stock liquidity

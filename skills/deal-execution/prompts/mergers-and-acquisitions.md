# Mergers & Acquisitions Advisory

## Role Context

```
You are a senior M&A advisor at a bulge-bracket investment bank. You have executed
dozens of sell-side auctions and buy-side acquisitions across industries. You think in
terms of strategic rationale, valuation triangulation, process dynamics, and deal
certainty. You know that every recommendation must be defensible in a board presentation
and that timing, confidentiality, and competitive tension drive outcomes. You are fluent
in merger agreements, regulatory frameworks, and cross-border structuring.
```

---

## What This Desk Does

M&A advisory sits at the center of investment banking. On the sell-side, the desk runs competitive auction processes designed to maximize value for the seller — from crafting teasers and CIMs to managing data rooms and evaluating final bids. On the buy-side, the team helps acquirers identify targets, structure offers, perform diligence, and negotiate definitive agreements. The work requires deep valuation expertise, strong process management, and the judgment to navigate board dynamics, regulatory hurdles, and stakeholder interests. Every deal is unique, but the analytical frameworks are consistent.

## 1. Sell-Side Process

### Teaser and CIM Development

```
I am running a sell-side process for [company name], a [description: e.g., $200M revenue
specialty chemical manufacturer] in [industry].

Key selling points:
- [Point 1: e.g., #2 market position in a $5B niche market]
- [Point 2: e.g., 30% EBITDA margins with 200bps of expansion opportunity]
- [Point 3: e.g., 90%+ recurring revenue from long-term contracts]
- [Point 4: e.g., Greenfield expansion project adding 40% capacity by 2027]

Financial summary:
- Revenue: $[X]M (LTM), growing [X]% organically
- EBITDA: $[X]M ([X]% margin)
- Capex: $[X]M (maintenance) + $[X]M (growth)
- Net debt: $[X]M

Help me structure:

1. **Teaser (1-page, blind profile)**:
   - Craft a compelling narrative without revealing the company's identity
   - Highlight the investment thesis in 3-4 bullet points
   - Include high-level financials (rounded to obscure identity)
   - What industry buzzwords and positioning language drive the most interest?

2. **Confidential Information Memorandum (CIM) outline**:
   - Executive summary and investment highlights
   - Industry overview with TAM, growth drivers, competitive landscape
   - Business description: products/services, customers, go-to-market
   - Financial overview: historical performance, margins, working capital, capex
   - Growth opportunities and management projections
   - How should I present the financial projections — management case only, or
     base/upside/downside?
```

### Bid Evaluation and Process Management

```
I am evaluating bids in a sell-side auction for [company name].

Process status: [Phase — e.g., final round with 3 bidders]

Bids received:
- Bidder A ([strategic/financial]): $[X]M enterprise value, [cash/stock/mix],
  [financing status], [key conditions]
- Bidder B ([strategic/financial]): $[X]M enterprise value, [cash/stock/mix],
  [financing status], [key conditions]
- Bidder C ([strategic/financial]): $[X]M enterprise value, [cash/stock/mix],
  [financing status], [key conditions]

Seller's EBITDA (LTM): $[X]M
Management case EBITDA (NTM): $[X]M

Help me:

1. **Normalize the bids**:
   - Convert all bids to implied EV/EBITDA (LTM and NTM)
   - Adjust for different EBITDA definitions (some bidders add back more than others)
   - Value any contingent consideration (earnouts) at probability-weighted NPV
   - Equity value = EV - Net Debt; implied price per share

2. **Assess deal certainty for each bid**:
   - Financing conditionality (committed financing > highly confident letters > uncommitted)
   - Regulatory risk (antitrust overlap, CFIUS/FDI, sector-specific approvals)
   - Due diligence remaining and timeline to signing
   - Board/shareholder approval requirements

3. **Recommend a winner** (or a strategy to extract more value):
   - Is there enough competitive tension to run a final "best and final" round?
   - Should we negotiate with the top bidder or create a two-horse race?
   - Value vs. certainty trade-off framework
```

## 2. Buy-Side Due Diligence & Valuation

### Strategic Rationale and Synergy Analysis

```
[Acquirer name] is considering the acquisition of [target name] for approximately
$[X]M enterprise value ([X]x LTM EBITDA).

Acquirer profile:
- Revenue: $[X]M, EBITDA: $[X]M
- Market position: [description]
- Strategic priorities: [e.g., geographic expansion, vertical integration, tech capability]

Target profile:
- Revenue: $[X]M, EBITDA: $[X]M
- Market position: [description]
- Key assets: [e.g., customer relationships, IP, distribution network]

Help me build the strategic rationale and synergy case:

1. **Strategic rationale** (the "why"):
   - Revenue synergies: cross-selling, geographic access, new channels
     Revenue synergy value = Incremental revenue x Margin x Probability of achievement
   - Cost synergies: headcount overlap, procurement savings, facility consolidation
     Cost synergy value = Run-rate savings x (1 - tax rate) / WACC (capitalized)
   - Financial synergies: tax attributes, lower blended cost of capital
   - Strategic value: market share, competitive positioning, technology

2. **Synergy quantification**:
   - Bottom-up synergy build by category
   - Phase-in timeline (typically 1-3 years to full run-rate)
   - One-time integration costs (restructuring, severance, IT migration)
   - Net synergy NPV = PV(run-rate synergies) - PV(integration costs)
   - What percentage of synergy value is the acquirer paying away in the premium?
     % paid away = (Premium / PV of synergies) x 100

3. **Integration risk assessment**:
   - Cultural compatibility, management retention, customer churn risk
   - Historical integration track record of the acquirer
   - What synergy achievement rate is realistic? (industry average: 60-80% of announced)
```

### Valuation Triangulation

```
I need to triangulate the valuation of [target company] using multiple methodologies
to support a buy-side recommendation.

Target financials:
- Revenue: $[X]M (LTM), $[X]M (NTM consensus)
- EBITDA: $[X]M (LTM), $[X]M (NTM)
- Unlevered FCF: $[X]M
- Net debt: $[X]M
- Revenue growth: [X]%
- EBITDA margin: [X]%

Provide a valuation summary using:

1. **DCF** (see ../roles/investment-banker.md for WACC and terminal value frameworks):
   - WACC range: [X]% to [X]%
   - Terminal growth: [X]% to [X]%
   - Implied EV range: $[X]M to $[X]M

2. **Trading comps**: [list 4-6 comparable public companies]
   - EV/EBITDA range, EV/Revenue range
   - Where should target trade within the range and why?

3. **Precedent transactions**: [list 3-5 relevant deals]
   - Implied EV/EBITDA, control premium paid

4. **LBO floor valuation** (what can a financial sponsor pay at 20%+ IRR?):
   - Entry leverage: [X]x, exit multiple: [X]x, hold period: [X] years
   - Maximum bid price = f(target IRR, debt capacity, exit assumptions)

5. **Football field chart**: summarize the implied EV range from each methodology
   and identify the overlap zone. What is the "fair" range?
```

## 3. Merger Model / Accretion-Dilution

### Pro Forma EPS and Financing Mix

```
Model the accretion/dilution impact of [Acquirer] acquiring [Target].

Acquirer:
- Share price: $[X], Shares outstanding: [X]M
- EPS (NTM): $[X], P/E: [X]x
- Pre-tax cost of debt: [X]%, Tax rate: [X]%
- Cash on hand: $[X]M

Target:
- Offer price: $[X] per share (equity value: $[X]M)
- EPS (NTM): $[X], Net income: $[X]M
- Net debt to be assumed: $[X]M

Expected synergies: $[X]M pre-tax (fully phased in Year 2)
Estimated intangible amortization: $[X]M/year over [X] years
Transaction costs: $[X]M (expensed in Year 1)

Calculate:

1. **Pro forma EPS under three financing scenarios**:
   Pro forma NI = Acquirer NI + Target NI + Synergies(1-t)
                  - New debt interest(1-t) - Intangible amortization(1-t)
                  + Lost cash interest(1-t) [opportunity cost of cash used]
   Pro forma shares = Acquirer shares + New shares issued (for stock portion)
   Pro forma EPS = Pro forma NI / Pro forma shares

   a) 100% cash (debt-financed)
   b) 100% stock (exchange ratio = Offer price / Acquirer share price)
   c) Proposed mix: [X]% cash / [X]% stock

2. **Break-even synergies**:
   Minimum synergies to be EPS-neutral:
   Break-even synergies = [Acquirer EPS x Pro forma shares - Acquirer NI - Target NI
                          + Financing costs(1-t) + Amortization(1-t)] / (1-t)

3. **Sensitivity matrix**: Accretion/dilution across:
   - Offer price (±10% from base)
   - Synergy realization (0%, 50%, 75%, 100%)
   - Cash/stock mix

4. **Year 1 vs. Year 2**: Show how accretion improves as synergies ramp
```

## 4. Fairness Opinion Analysis

### Fairness Assessment Framework

```
The board of [company name] has received a proposed acquisition offer at $[X] per share
(implied equity value: $[X]M, implied EV: $[X]M, implied EV/EBITDA: [X]x).

The board has asked: "Is this price fair from a financial point of view?"

Company financials:
- Revenue (LTM): $[X]M, (NTM): $[X]M
- EBITDA (LTM): $[X]M, (NTM): $[X]M
- Net income: $[X]M
- Unlevered FCF: $[X]M
- Net debt: $[X]M
- 52-week price range: $[X] to $[X]
- Unaffected share price (before rumors): $[X]
- Implied premium to unaffected: [X]%

Structure a fairness analysis:

1. **Valuation summary** (implied range from each method):
   - DCF: $[X] to $[X] per share
   - Trading comps: $[X] to $[X] per share
   - Precedent transactions: $[X] to $[X] per share
   - 52-week trading range and VWAP analysis
   - Analyst target prices (low/median/high)

2. **Is the offer within the "fair" range?**
   - Does the offer price fall within the overlap of methodologies?
   - Is the premium consistent with precedent premiums in the industry?
   - Premium analysis: Offer / Unaffected price - 1 = [X]%
     Compare to: median premium for comparable deals

3. **Financial analysis considerations**:
   - Present value of status quo (management plan DCF without a sale)
   - Is the company better off as a standalone entity?
   - Are there other potential buyers who might pay more?
   - Sum-of-the-parts: is the whole being sold for less than the parts?

4. **Process considerations** (qualitative, but informs fairness):
   - Was the process sufficiently competitive?
   - Were go-shop provisions negotiated?
   - Any conflicts of interest (management buyout, related party)?
```

## 5. Cross-Border M&A Considerations

### International Deal Structuring

```
[Acquirer country: e.g., US-based] acquirer is buying [target country: e.g., German]
target for [currency] [X]M enterprise value.

Acquirer details:
- Jurisdiction: [X], Functional currency: [X]
- Tax rate: [X]%, Existing international operations: [Y/N]

Target details:
- Jurisdiction: [X], Functional currency: [X]
- Revenue: [X]M [local currency], EBITDA: [X]M [local currency]
- Employees: [X], Key regulatory licenses: [list]

Help me analyze cross-border considerations:

1. **FX risk and valuation impact**:
   - Current spot rate: [X], Forward rate (deal close): [X]
   - Valuation in both currencies at spot and forward
   - FX hedging strategy: forward contracts, options, natural hedge
   - Post-close translation risk: How does target EBITDA contribute to
     consolidated results under FX volatility?
   - Translation impact = Target EBITDA x (Spot - Budget rate) / Budget rate

2. **Tax structuring**:
   - Optimal acquisition structure (share deal vs. asset deal)
   - Holding company jurisdiction (e.g., Netherlands, Luxembourg, Ireland)
   - Interest deductibility limits (e.g., ATAD in EU, Section 163(j) in US)
   - Withholding tax on dividends, interest, royalties (treaty network)
   - Step-up opportunities and goodwill amortization by jurisdiction
   - Repatriation strategy for target's cash flows

3. **Regulatory approvals timeline**:
   - Antitrust: [list jurisdictions with filings required — e.g., EC, FTC/DOJ, SAMR]
   - Foreign direct investment screening (CFIUS, EU FDI framework)
   - Industry-specific regulators (telecom, banking, defense, energy)
   - Expected timeline from signing to closing: [X] months
   - Reverse break fee and regulatory ticking fees

4. **Cultural and operational integration**:
   - Labor law differences (works councils, co-determination, severance rules)
   - Local management retention strategy
   - Data privacy (GDPR, cross-border data transfer restrictions)

5. **Financing in local currency**:
   - Should the acquirer raise debt in the target's currency?
   - Natural hedge: local currency debt against local currency EBITDA
   - Comparative cost of debt across jurisdictions
```

## Mathematical Frameworks

Building on the foundations in [`../roles/investment-banker.md`](../roles/investment-banker.md):

**Synergy-Adjusted Valuation**:
- Maximum bid = Standalone value + PV(synergies) - Integration costs
- Value creation for acquirer = PV(synergies) - Premium paid
- % of synergies retained = 1 - (Premium / PV(synergies))

**Exchange Ratio Mechanics** (stock-for-stock):
- Exchange ratio = Offer price per target share / Acquirer share price
- New shares issued = Target shares x Exchange ratio
- Pro forma ownership: Acquirer % = Acquirer shares / (Acquirer shares + New shares)

**Control Premium Decomposition**:
- Total premium = Standalone re-rating + Synergy sharing + Overbid
- Fair premium = PV(synergies) x Buyer's willingness to share / Target equity value

**Earnout Valuation**:
- Earnout PV = Sum of [P(milestone_i achieved) x Payment_i / (1 + r)^t_i]
- Use higher discount rate (12-18%) reflecting contingent nature

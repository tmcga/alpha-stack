# UHNW Private Banking Advisory

## Role Context

```
You are a senior private banker and UHNW advisory specialist with 20+ years of experience
serving clients with $25M+ in investable assets. You have deep expertise in custom lending
solutions, structured products, concentrated stock monetization, family office governance,
and multi-jurisdictional wealth planning. You hold the CFA, CFP, and CTFA designations.
You communicate with precision and discretion, always considering tax efficiency, estate
implications, and risk management in every recommendation. You never provide final legal
or tax advice but frame solutions for discussion with the client's counsel and tax advisor.
```

## What This Desk Does

The UHNW private banking team serves as the central relationship hub for ultra-high-net-worth families, typically those with $25 million or more in investable assets. The desk coordinates across lending, investments, trust and estate, tax advisory, and insurance to deliver holistic balance-sheet management. Unlike retail wealth management, private banking emphasizes bespoke solutions -- custom credit facilities, structured hedging, multi-entity governance, and cross-border coordination. The goal is long-term family relationship management across generations, not transactional product sales.

## 2. Custom Lending Solutions

Bespoke credit facilities leveraging the client's full balance sheet -- securities, real estate, art, aircraft, and other collateral.

**Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratios by Collateral Type:**

| Collateral | Typical LTV | Margin Call Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| US Large Cap Equities | 50-70% | LTV > 75-80% |
| Investment Grade Bonds | 70-85% | LTV > 90% |
| Municipal Bonds | 65-80% | LTV > 85% |
| Concentrated Single Stock | 30-50% | LTV > 55-65% |
| Hedge Fund Interests | 0-40% | Varies by liquidity |
| Real Estate (primary) | 60-75% | N/A (amortizing) |
| Art / Collectibles | 30-50% | Appraised value decline |
| Aircraft / Yacht | 50-70% | N/A (amortizing) |

**Margin Call Mechanics:**

When LTV exceeds the maintenance threshold:
- Cure period: typically 3-5 business days
- Options: post additional collateral, pay down principal, or forced liquidation
- Maintenance LTV = Loan Balance / Current Collateral Value
- Collateral shortfall = Loan Balance - (Maintenance LTV x Current Collateral Value)

### Securities-Backed Lending

```
A client requests a $[loan amount] credit facility secured by their investment portfolio
valued at $[portfolio value]. The portfolio consists of:

- [X]% US large-cap equities (diversified)
- [X]% investment-grade fixed income
- [X]% municipal bonds
- [X]% single stock ([ticker])
- [X]% hedge fund interests (quarterly redemption)

Structure a securities-backed line of credit (SBLOC) including:
1. Blended LTV calculation: sum of (asset class weight x asset class LTV advance rate)
2. Maximum borrowing capacity at initial and maintenance LTV levels
3. Stress test: at what portfolio decline level does a margin call trigger?
4. Interest rate structure: [SOFR/Prime] + spread based on loan size and relationship
5. Tax efficiency analysis: borrowing vs. selling (interest deduction vs. capital gains)
6. Risk warnings the advisor must disclose under Reg BI
```

## 3. Structured Solutions for UHNW

Customized hedging and monetization strategies for concentrated positions and complex wealth structures.

**Key Strategies and Their Tax Treatment:**

| Strategy | Mechanism | Tax Event? | Typical Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Equity Collar | Buy put + sell call | Deferred (no sale) | Downside protection while retaining upside |
| Prepaid Variable Forward (PVF) | Forward sale with upfront cash | Deferred to settlement | Monetization without current tax |
| Exchange Fund | Contribute stock, receive diversified interest | Deferred (7yr hold) | Diversification for low-basis stock |
| Charitable Remainder Trust | Donate stock to CRT | Partial deduction; tax-free sale inside trust | Charitable intent + income stream |
| 10b5-1 Plan | Prescheduled sales | Taxable at each sale | Systematic diversification for insiders |

**Collar Economics:**

- Protective put cost: pay premium for [strike] put
- Covered call income: receive premium for [strike] call
- Zero-cost collar: put strike and call strike set so premiums offset
- Net position: locked between put strike (floor) and call strike (cap)
- Prepaid variable forward: collar + loan economics combined

### Equity Collar and Prepaid Variable Forward

```
A client holds [shares] shares of [ticker] at $[current price] with a cost basis of
$[basis]. They want to:
- Protect against downside below $[floor price]
- Access $[liquidity need] in cash within [timeframe]
- Defer capital gains recognition for [X] years

Design and compare:
1. Zero-cost equity collar:
   - Put strike at [X]% below current price
   - Call strike that zeroes the net premium
   - Collar band width and effective upside participation
   - Constructive sale risk analysis under IRC Section 1259

2. Prepaid variable forward:
   - Upfront cash as percentage of notional (typically 75-90%)
   - Implied borrowing rate: (notional - upfront) / upfront / term
   - Settlement options: physical delivery vs. cash
   - Tax deferral mechanics and when gain is recognized

3. Comparison matrix: collar vs. PVF vs. outright sale vs. margin loan
   Include: after-tax proceeds, ongoing exposure, liquidity, complexity, counterparty risk
```

### Exchange Fund Analysis

```
A client has $[value] in highly appreciated [ticker] shares (basis: $[basis], holding
period: [long-term/short-term]). They are considering contributing to an exchange fund.

Analyze the exchange fund strategy:
1. Eligibility: qualified client status, minimum contribution, diversification test
2. Mechanics: contribute appreciated stock, receive pro-rata interest in diversified pool
3. Tax deferral: basis carries over; no gain until fund interest is sold/redeemed
4. Seven-year holding requirement under IRC Section 721(c) and 731
5. Fees: management fee [X]%, carried interest [X]%, lock-up premium
6. Comparison of after-tax terminal wealth:
   - Hold concentrated position (risk-adjusted)
   - Sell and reinvest (after capital gains tax)
   - Exchange fund (fee-adjusted, diversified, tax-deferred)
7. Risks: illiquidity, manager selection, tracking to desired allocation, regulatory change
```

## 5. Cross-Border Considerations

Tax treaties, regulatory compliance, and multi-jurisdictional planning for internationally mobile UHNW clients.

**Key Regulatory Frameworks:**

- **FATCA** (Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act): US persons must report foreign financial assets; foreign institutions must report US account holders
- **CRS** (Common Reporting Standard): OECD automatic exchange of financial account information across 100+ jurisdictions
- **FBAR** (FinCEN 114): US persons with foreign accounts exceeding $10,000 aggregate
- **PFIC** rules: punitive US tax treatment of passive foreign investment companies
- **Exit tax** (IRC 877A): mark-to-market tax on covered expatriates

### Multi-Jurisdiction Planning

```
A client is a [nationality] citizen residing in [country], with:
- US green card / citizenship / substantial presence: [yes/no]
- Assets in: [list jurisdictions -- US, UK, Switzerland, Singapore, etc.]
- Real estate in: [jurisdictions]
- Business interests in: [jurisdictions]
- Family members who are tax residents of: [jurisdictions]
- Planned relocation to [jurisdiction] within [timeframe]

Analyze the cross-border planning considerations:
1. Tax residency determination for each jurisdiction (days test, domicile, center of life)
2. Applicable tax treaties and their impact on:
   - Investment income (dividends, interest, capital gains)
   - Real estate income and gains
   - Trust and estate taxation
3. FATCA/CRS reporting obligations for all accounts
4. Entity structuring: which holding structures are tax-efficient across jurisdictions?
5. Pre-immigration / pre-emigration planning checklist
6. Forced heirship rules vs. common-law freedom of disposition
7. Recommended professional team (tax counsel per jurisdiction, cross-border estate attorney)
```

### Offshore Structure Review

```
A client has the following offshore structure established [X] years ago:
- [Entity type] in [jurisdiction] holding $[value] in assets
- [Trust type] in [jurisdiction] with [beneficiaries]
- [Company/fund] in [jurisdiction] for [purpose]

Conduct a structure review considering:
1. Current tax compliance status (has the structure been properly reported?)
2. Substance requirements in each jurisdiction (economic substance rules)
3. Beneficial ownership registries and transparency requirements
4. Cost-benefit analysis: is the structure still tax-efficient given recent law changes?
5. Simplification opportunities that reduce compliance burden without tax cost
6. Voluntary disclosure considerations if any reporting gaps exist
```
